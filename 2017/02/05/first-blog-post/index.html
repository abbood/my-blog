Welcome aboard! Let’s get to know one each other

My name is Abdullah Bakhach, I’m the founder of Lobo Labs (which is a huge conglomerate of companies, but I’m also maybe the sole employee of it you never know). I have a passion for learning new stuff and in turn teaching others. I’m specifically passionate about the software field and fitness.

This blog is more of a Lobo Labs blog, so I will be posting stuff that’s relevant to the company sometimes, and sometimes I’ll just post some random rants related to my passions. I believe I’ll mostly be talking about productivity at work (which can be useful for most practitioners, not just software engineers) and also about parallels between productivity at the workplace and fitness principles. My goal is that for each post, the reader should walk away with something useful and applicable; something they can apply to their lives almost immediately.

In general I usually have a lot to say and share. Sometimes I say things as a reminder to myself: I think of it almost as a personal journal ie what was I thinking back in ‘12 when I was doing such and such?

A little bit more about me, personally speaking

I’m a proud husband and father of two (My son Hassan is in the front page pic, he is about 3.5 years old, and I got a daughter, Noorah). I was born in the US, spent my childhood in Saudi Arabia, moved to Canada when I was 14 and then moved back to the States 12 years later. It has always been my dream to move back to Lebanon, and so I finally made the jump and left Amazon where I was working as a software engineer and moved to Lebanon back in 2012. I made an arrangement with a Canadian startup (sweet tooth) to work remotely from Lebanon. That worked perfectly! Only 3 months later I got fired b/c I was “a terrible software engineer”. That was a painful lesson, but also a huge motivation to become a better software engineer. I worked for some Lebanese start-ups and spent a lot of time on Stack Overflow to improve my skills.

It was Stack Overflow that introduced me to a Brazilian client, who liked one of my posts and asked me to pull together a remote team. I did that and in doing so created Lobo Labs. We soon published our first product, Vibereel.

Lobo Labs has gone through several pivots since then. After Vibereel (and one more other client) I worked for Mokus.io as COO, scaling the remote team and setting up processes to manage them, mostly using the experience I got while managing the team at Vibereel.

After Mokus I’m currently working as a consultant for Lebanese Start-ups, specifically in the area of setting up processes and scaling their teams. I’m also working on my own (stealth) product in the meantime, which is still in the online productivity space.
